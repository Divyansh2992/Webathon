    What is water sustainability?

    Water sustainability is the holistic management of water resources to meet present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to access clean water. It involves efficient resource management, emphasizing conservation, and the protection of water quality and ecosystems. This requires a balance between human water usage and the needs of the environment. Through community engagement, cross-sectoral collaboration, and effective governance, water sustainability can be achieved. Policies and regulations play a crucial role in promoting responsible water management practices, while investment in innovation and infrastructure is essential for long-term resilience.

    Sustainable water solutions: the basics

    Sustainable water systems should provide adequate water quantity and appropriate water quality for a given need, without compromising the future ability to provide this capacity and quality, according to the IWA.

    The association states: “Water systems in the realm of sustainable development may not literally include the use of water, but include systems where the use of water has traditionally been required. Examples include waterless toilets and waterless car washes, whose use helps to alleviate water stress and secure sustainable water supply.”

    Sustainable water solutions form the cornerstone of responsible resource management, addressing the pressing need to ensure the availability and quality of water for current and future generations.

    Surface water

    Surface water management is pivotal for water sustainability, offering renewable freshwater sources through rivers, lakes, and reservoirs. When managed effectively, surface water systems contribute to ecological balance, supporting biodiversity and habitat preservation. Communities rely on surface water for drinking, agriculture, industry, and recreation, underscoring its significance. Sustainable practices such as watershed protection, pollution prevention, and water conservation are imperative to maintain surface water quality and quantity. Additionally, surface water management plays a crucial role in flood control, regulating water flow and mitigating flood risks

    Groundwater

    Groundwater accounts for more than 50 per cent of global freshwater and is critical for potable water. Groundwater can be a sustainable supply of water only if the amount of water entering, leaving and being stored in the system is conserved. The IWA says unsustainable groundwater use results in water-level decline, reduce streamflow and low quality water, directly impacting local communities.

    Reclaimed water

    Reclaimed water, or water reuse as it’s known, can also be a sustainable source for water supply and can alleviate
    stress on primary water resources, such as surface and groundwater. Depending on the given application,
    reclaimed water must be treated to provide an appropriate quality, for example for irrigation or industry use. The
    level of water reuse varies globally. Countries such as Spain and China continue to lead the way in water reuse,
    with the later accounting for 49 percent of capacity contracted between 2010 and 2017.
